The Popularity and Growth of Anime

What is anime?

Anime is a style of animation that originates from Japan and has grown into a global phenomenon. It is known for its diverse storytelling, unique art styles and appeal to various age groups.. This page aims to explore the popularity and growth of anime from 2023 - 2025 with the use of multiple visualisations, both globally and in Australia

The number of searches is estimated using search index data from Google Trends and estimated population data from the World Bank.

Countries with no data are shown in grey.

To observe the trends in anime popularity over time, we can look at the chart on the right.

The chart shows the daily anime search index from Google Trends from 2023 to 2024. Data from 2025 was omitted as it is incomplete.

Observe that in both years, there is a gradual increase in anime searches over from Jun to mid-July, before slowly dropping off again. this is because of the summer anime season, where many new anime series are released.

However, the interest in anime during 2024 seems to be lower than that of 2023 in general, with the exception of a few peaks in interest. This could be due to a variety of factors, such as the quality of anime released, competition from other forms of entertainment, or changes in audience preferences.

Anime Genres and Their Ratings

The chart above shows the top 30 anime series and their associated genres.

We can see that some anime series have a large number of genres associated with them, while others have only a few. This suggests that some anime series are more complex and multifaceted than others, which may contribute to their popularity.

The lolipop chart also shows the top 10 most common genres among the top 30 anime series. We can see that "Comedy" is the most common genre, followed by "Action" and "Fantasy". These popular genres seem to line up with the top 10 highest rated anime series, which also feature a lot of "Comedy", "Action" and "Fantasy" genres, and are highligheted in the first chart.

The scatter plot on the left shows the relationship between an anime's ratings and their popularity.

Note that the ratings score reflects an individuals viewer's opinion of the anime, from a scale of 1 to 10. The popularity score is a measure of how many people have watched the anime or have it added to their watchlist.

The chart suggests that whle certain anime gain significant attention and a large following, they may not necessarily be highly rated by viewers.

This could be due to a variety of factors, such as the anime's marketing, hype, or the presence of popular voice actors or directors. Currently trending genres or mainstream appeal could also play a role in an anime's popularity, even if it is not highly rated.

The data raises interesting questions about the relationship between mass appeal and perceived quality of anime within the community.

Anime Viewership Demographics

The charts above show the distribution average age of anime viewers around the world.

We observe that the average age of anime viewers is between ages 39 to 42, with France having the lowest average age of 39, and Japan having the highest average age of 41.3. This makes sense as Japan is the birthplace of anime, and has a more established and older fanbase. France has a younger average age, which could be due to the fact that anime is more popular among younger generations in France.

We can also notice that there are, in general, more female viewers of anime than male viewers, with the exception of Japan, where there are more male viewers than female viewers. This could be due to cultural differences in the perception of anime and its target audience. In Japan, anime is often seen as a male-dominated medium, while in other countries, it is more widely accepted and enjoyed by both genders.

This information suggests that anime appeals to a wide range of age groups, and is not limited to a specific demographic.
